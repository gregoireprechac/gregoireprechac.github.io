About

At the intersection
of brain and thought.

I am a Neuro-X master student at EPFL, working between neuroscience, philosophy, and the conceptual questions that connect them. In Prof. Herzog's laboratory, my thesis research involves designing and running fMRI-EEG experiments on inter-individual differences in neural signals. Alongside that, I am working on a paper examining the axiomatic foundations of Integrated Information Theory and its epistemological consequences.

Philosophy has always shaped how I approach science. Since 2022, I run the EPFL Philosophy Association, organising conferences and discussions on ethics, consciousness, and the boundary between philosophy and science. I co-founded the Swiss Student Chopin Contest and have been playing piano for thirteen years.

Outside neuroscience, I read widely in politics, literature, and history. I think that breadth makes better science.
